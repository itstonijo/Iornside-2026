# Startup Checklist — Starting Revhead Post in Australia

*Every step to go from idea to legally trading, with official links. Figures are
current for the 2026–27 financial year (from 1 July 2026). Fees change yearly —
always confirm on the linked official site. This is general information, not
legal or tax advice; for your specific situation, speak to an accountant.*

---

## Phase 1 — Decide your business structure

You have two realistic starting options:

### Option A — Sole trader (recommended to start) ✅
- **Cost:** free to set up (just an ABN).
- **Pros:** cheapest, simplest, fastest; you report business income on your
  personal tax return; easy to run solo.
- **Cons:** you are personally liable for business debts; the business isn't a
  separate legal entity.
- **Best when:** you're testing the idea, bootstrapping, and turnover is modest.

### Option B — Company (Pty Ltd)
- **Cost:** **$636 one-off** ASIC registration (from 1 Jul 2026) + **$342/yr**
  ASIC annual review fee. Accountant setup often $500–1,000 more.
- **Pros:** limited liability (protects personal assets), looks more credible,
  easier to bring on partners/investors, flat company tax rate.
- **Cons:** more cost, more paperwork, annual ASIC review, separate tax return.
- **Best when:** the business is proven, turnover is growing toward/past $75k,
  or you want liability protection.

> **Recommendation:** Start as a **sole trader**. Revisit moving to a Pty Ltd
> around the point you approach $75k turnover or want liability protection
> (covered in the 12-month plan). Changing structure later is normal and
> straightforward.

**Compare structures:**
<https://business.gov.au/planning/business-structures-and-types/business-structures>

## Phase 2 — Register (the legal essentials)

### ☐ 2.1 Get an Australian Business Number (ABN) — **FREE**
Every Australian business needs one. Apply as a sole trader; you'll get a TFN
prompt too.
- Apply: <https://business.gov.au/registrations/register-for-an-australian-business-number-abn>
- Or via the Australian Business Register: <https://www.abr.gov.au>
- **Cost: $0.** Beware third-party sites that charge a fee for this — it's free
  from the government.

### ☐ 2.2 Register your business name — **$47/yr or $108/3yr**
Required if you trade under anything other than your own personal name (you
will — "Revhead Post"). Registered through ASIC, done via business.gov.au.
- Register: <https://business.gov.au/registrations/register-your-business-name>
- **First check the name is available** (business name, domain, and trademark):
  <https://connectonline.asic.gov.au> and search the trademark register (below).
- **Cost:** $47 (1 year) or $108 (3 years) from 1 July 2026.

### ☐ 2.3 Check trademark availability — **FREE to search**
Before you commit to "Revhead Post," search IP Australia's register to make sure
no one already owns a conflicting trademark, and consider registering your own.
- Search: <https://search.ipaustralia.gov.au/trademarks/search/quick>
- Registering a trademark (optional but recommended once you're committed):
  from ~$250/class via TM Headstart. See [LEGAL-AND-IP.md](LEGAL-AND-IP.md).

### ☐ 2.4 Register a Tax File Number (TFN)
As a sole trader you use your **personal TFN**. (A company needs its own TFN,
applied for with the ABN.) Nothing extra to do here if sole trader.

### ☐ 2.5 GST — register only when required
- You **must** register for GST once your turnover is **$75,000/yr or more**
  (or if you expect to reach it). Below that it's optional.
- GST registration is **free** via the ATO / Business Portal.
- Info: <https://www.ato.gov.au/businesses-and-organisations/gst-excise-and-indirect-taxes/gst/registering-for-gst>
- **Recommendation:** don't register for GST until you have to — below the
  threshold it just adds 10% admin and doesn't help a consumer business.

## Phase 3 — Money & banking

### ☐ 3.1 Open a separate business bank account
Not legally required for a sole trader, but strongly recommended (clean books,
easier tax). Required for a company. Most AU banks offer $0 monthly business
transaction accounts. Look at Up, Commbank, NAB, Westpac, ANZ, or Macquarie.

### ☐ 3.2 Set up a payment processor
Your Shopify store will use **Shopify Payments** (Stripe under the hood) or
**Stripe** directly. Fees ~1.75% + $0.30 per domestic card transaction.
- Shopify Payments: built into Shopify.
- Stripe AU: <https://stripe.com/au>

### ☐ 3.3 Bookkeeping
Start with a simple spreadsheet; move to **Xero** (~$35/mo) once you have steady
volume. Keep every receipt (deductible: printing, postage, Shopify, ads, home
office, etc.). <https://www.xero.com/au/>

## Phase 4 — Build the product & store

### ☐ 4.1 Buy your domain
- `.com.au` requires an ABN (you now have one). Register through an auDA-accredited
  registrar (VentraIP, Crazy Domains, GoDaddy AU). ~$20/yr.
- auDA (the .au authority): <https://www.auda.org.au>
- Grab the matching `.com` and social handles too.

### ☐ 4.2 Build the store on Shopify + a subscription app
- Shopify AU Basic: ~$51/mo (or ~$5/mo "Starter" if you want to test cheaply).
  <https://www.shopify.com/au>
- Subscription app (for recurring billing): **Recharge**, **Loop**, or
  **Seal Subscriptions** (Seal has a free tier). Install from the Shopify App
  Store.
- Alternative all-in-one: **Subbly** is purpose-built for subscription boxes.
  <https://www.subbly.co>
- Use the landing page in [`site/index.html`](site/index.html) as your brand
  starting point / waitlist page while the store is being built.

### ☐ 4.3 Line up a printer & packaging
See the full list in [SUPPLIERS-AND-TOOLS.md](SUPPLIERS-AND-TOOLS.md). Order a
**sample drop** first and hold it in your hand before you sell anything.

### ☐ 4.4 Set up shipping with Australia Post
- Open a **Business account / MyPost Business** for cheaper rates and easy
  labels: <https://auspost.com.au/business>
- Design each drop to fit the **Large Letter** band (≤125g, ≤260×360mm,
  ≤20mm thick) to keep postage cheap. Confirm current rates:
  <https://auspost.com.au/sending/send-within-australia/letters>

## Phase 5 — Protect yourself & stay compliant

### ☐ 5.1 Understand Australian Consumer Law (ACL) for subscriptions
Subscriptions with auto-renewal have specific rules: clear disclosure of price
and billing frequency, no pre-ticked consent, and an easy way to cancel. Read
[LEGAL-AND-IP.md](LEGAL-AND-IP.md) §Consumer law. ACCC guidance:
<https://www.accc.gov.au/business>

### ☐ 5.2 Write your policies
You need: **Terms of Service**, **Privacy Policy** (required if you collect
personal data), **Shipping Policy**, **Refund/Returns Policy**, and clear
**subscription/cancellation terms**. Shopify has templates; have them reviewed
if budget allows (Sprintlaw/LawPath do affordable AU packages).

### ☐ 5.3 Privacy
If turnover is under $3M you may be exempt from parts of the Privacy Act, but
best practice (and Shopify/Stripe requirements) is to have a privacy policy and
handle data responsibly. <https://www.oaic.gov.au>

### ☐ 5.4 Insurance (optional early, sensible as you grow)
Consider **public liability** and **product liability** cover. Cheap while
small; brokers like BizCover quote online. <https://www.bizcover.com.au>

### ☐ 5.5 Business insurance & super
As a sole trader you're not obliged to pay yourself super, but set some aside.
If you ever hire, you take on PAYG withholding, super guarantee, and workers'
comp obligations.
