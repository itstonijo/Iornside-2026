# Business Plan — Revhead Post

*A monthly car-culture subscription box for Australia. Last updated: July 2026.*

---

## 1. The concept

Petrol enthusiasts love the **stories, art and nostalgia** of great cars far more
than they'll ever be able to own them. Revhead Post is a **physical monthly
mail club**: for a low monthly fee, subscribers receive a beautifully designed
envelope built around one surprise "hero car." Inside each drop:

- **3 × Polaroid-style photo prints** of the featured car
- **1 × A5 artwork print** (poster-quality)
- **1 × blueprint / cutaway print**
- **1 × collector trading card** (stats on the back — build a set over time)
- **1 × story letter** — the history, the myth, why the car matters

The surprise is the hook: you don't know which car is coming until it lands in
your letterbox on the 1st of the month. It's cheap enthusiast joy, collectible,
and highly giftable.

This is a direct adaptation of the UK's [Petrol Post](https://petrolpost.com)
model, localised for Australia (pricing in AUD, Australia Post fulfilment,
Australian Consumer Law compliance, and an Aussie car-culture editorial voice —
Bathurst, Summernats, Holden vs Ford, Skylines, utes, the lot).

---

## 2. Why this works as a solo / bootstrapped business

- **Recurring revenue** — subscriptions give predictable monthly cash flow.
- **Tiny startup cost** — under $150 to be legally trading; print-on-demand
  means you don't hold much stock.
- **High gross margin** — ~65–70% after materials and postage (see
  [FINANCIAL-MODEL.md](FINANCIAL-MODEL.md)).
- **Lightweight fulfilment** — everything fits in a "Large Letter" through
  Australia Post; no pallets, no warehouse.
- **Content is the product** — your edge is taste, design and storytelling, not
  capital.
- **Giftable & seasonal** — Father's Day, Christmas, birthdays drive spikes.

---

## 3. Market

- Australia has one of the highest per-capita rates of car ownership and a deep
  motorsport / car-culture following (Supercars, Summernats, cars-and-coffee
  scenes, huge JDM and classic communities).
- Comparable enthusiast subscription boxes exist and sustain themselves on a few
  thousand subscribers globally; you only need **a few hundred** in Australia to
  run a healthy solo business.
- **Target customer:** men and women 25–55, disposable income, follow car pages
  on Instagram/TikTok/YouTube, nostalgic for the cars they grew up with, and
  buy car merch, posters, models and magazines.
- **Also a gift market:** partners and kids buying for the "petrolhead who has
  everything."

### Positioning
> *"Every month, one legendary car lands in your letterbox."*
Premium design, surprise format, collectible over time, unmistakably Australian.

|------|-------------|----------|

tier with an extra diecast or enamel pin, and a full trading-card binder.

## 5. How it runs each month (operations)

1. **Weeks 1–2:** choose next month's hero car; source/commission imagery
   (see legal note); design the 6 inserts in Canva/Illustrator.
2. **Week 3:** send print files to the printer; order envelopes/packaging.
3. **Week 4:** prints arrive; you (or a fulfilment helper) collate and stuff
   envelopes; print address labels from Shopify.
4. **1st of the month:** lodge with Australia Post as Large Letters.
5. **Ongoing:** answer support email, post to socials 3–5×/week, manage the
   waitlist and gift orders.

Time cost: realistically **1–2 days of design + 1 day of fulfilment per month**
at small scale. Outsource fulfilment (pick-pack) once you pass ~200 subscribers.

## 6. Competitive moat

You can't patent a subscription box, so the defensibility is:
- **Brand & editorial voice** — a distinctly Australian car-culture personality.
- **Design quality** — objectively beautiful prints people want to keep/frame.
- **The collectible mechanic** — the trading-card set and back-issues create
  switching cost (subscribers don't want a gap in their collection).
- **Community** — a private group, member-voted cars, member photos featured.
- **Licensing relationships** — over time, official licences or partnerships
  with clubs, photographers and marques others can't easily replicate.

## 7. Key risks (and mitigations)

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| **Image/trademark infringement** (biggest) | Commission original artwork, licence photos, or use own photography + brand-neutral blueprints. See [LEGAL-AND-IP.md](LEGAL-AND-IP.md). |
| High churn (subscribers cancel) | Nail design quality; annual prepay; collectible set creates stickiness. |
| Postage cost creep | Keep each drop under the Large-Letter weight/thickness band; review Australia Post pricing yearly. |
| Slow customer acquisition | Organic short-form video is the channel (see marketing plan); build a waitlist before launch. |
| Founder time / burnout | Batch design; outsource fulfilment early; build a 2–3 month content buffer. |
| Consumer-law breaches on auto-renewal | Clear pre-tick-free consent, easy cancellation, honest advertising (ACL). See legal doc. |

## 8. 12-month plan

- **Month 0 (now):** register ABN + business name; buy domain; build store;
  print samples; publish landing page + waitlist; design first 3 drops.
- **Months 1–2:** soft-launch to waitlist + friends; get to ~50 subscribers;
  gather testimonials and photos of real drops.
- **Months 3–6:** organic content engine (Reels/TikTok/YouTube Shorts) 4–5×/wk;
  first paid ads test; introduce gift tiers; aim ~200 subscribers.
- **Months 6–9:** outsource fulfilment; add collector tier + back-issues;
  Father's Day + Christmas gift push; aim ~400 subscribers.
- **Months 9–12:** review structure — if profitable and turnover approaching
  $75k, consider moving to a Pty Ltd company and registering for GST; pursue a
  licensing partnership or two. Aim ~600+ subscribers.

**North-star metric:** net active subscribers. Secondary: monthly churn %,
customer-acquisition cost (CAC), and contribution margin per box.
